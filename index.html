What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is like teaching computers to think and make decisions the way humans do. Instead of following pre-written instructions, AI systems can learn from experience, recognize patterns, and solve problems on their own.

Key AI Concepts

🧠

Machine Learning

Teaching computers to learn from data and improve over time without being explicitly programmed for every task.

🕸️

Neural Networks

Computer systems inspired by the human brain, made of connected nodes that process information together.

👁️

Computer Vision

Teaching computers to understand and interpret visual information from images and videos, just like human sight.

💬

Natural Language Processing

Helping computers understand, interpret, and generate human language in a meaningful way.

🚗

Deep Learning

Advanced machine learning using deep neural networks to solve complex problems like self-driving cars.

⚖️

AI Ethics

Ensuring AI is developed and used responsibly, fairly, and safely for the benefit of all humanity.

AI Through History

1950 - The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposed a test to determine if a machine can think like a human by having conversations that are indistinguishable from a human.

1956 - Birth of AI

The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at Dartmouth College, marking the official beginning of AI as a field of study.

1997 - Deep Blue Wins

IBM's Deep Blue became the first computer to defeat a world chess champion, showing AI could master complex strategic games.

2010s - Deep Learning Revolution

Advances in neural networks led to breakthroughs in image recognition, speech processing, and natural language understanding.

2020s - AI Goes Mainstream

AI assistants, recommendation systems, and generative AI became part of everyday life for millions of people worldwide.
